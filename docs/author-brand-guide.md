# Author Brand Guide

## Project

Eric Deloach Author Website

## Purpose of This Document

This document defines the author brand direction for Eric Deloach’s website.

It should guide all copy, visuals, page structure, CTAs, metadata, book positioning, and social integration across the site.

## Canonical Author Name

Working public name:

```text
Eric Deloach
```

Known name variations across platforms may include:

```text
Eric DeLoach
Eric De’Loach
ERIC DELOACH
Eric Loko
```

Use **Eric Deloach** consistently across the website unless the client approves a different canonical display name.

Use alternate name variations only where useful for SEO, source references, social handles, metadata, or profile matching.

## Author Brand Summary

Eric Deloach writes dark fantasy, crime, science fiction, thrillers, and gritty action stories where power, loyalty, vengeance, redemption, and humanity collide.

His work moves between two distinct but connected creative lanes:

1. Epic mythological fantasy
2. Grounded action and military thrillers

Across both lanes, Eric’s signature style is intense, cinematic, character-driven storytelling built around impossible choices and people pushed to their physical, emotional, and moral limits.

## Brand Voice

The brand voice should be:

* Cinematic
* Intense
* Confident
* Dark
* Direct
* Genre-aware

* Professional
* Emotionally charged without being melodramatic

## Writing Style

Use writing that feels sharp, immersive, and clear.

The copy should sound like it belongs on a professional author website for fantasy, thriller, and action readers.

Preferred style:

* Strong verbs
* Clear stakes
* Short to medium paragraph lengths
* Direct reader pathways
* Dramatic but controlled language
* Concrete story hooks
* Genre-specific terms
* Clean CTA language

Avoid:

* Generic author website language
* Fake hype
* Overly academic phrasing
* Long, dense paragraphs
* Excessive adjectives
* Spoilers beyond approved synopsis details
* Unverified claims
* Fake reviews or praise
* Overexplaining the plot

## Copy Rule

Do not use em dashes in site copy.

Use commas, periods, parentheses, colons, or semicolons instead.

## Visual Identity Direction

The site’s visual identity should balance both genre lanes.

Overall style:

* Dark, polished, cinematic
* Strong contrast
* Clean typography
* Editorial book presentation
* Premium author platform feel
* Modern but not sterile
* Dramatic but readable

Suggested visual split:

```text
Fantasy lane:
Fire, shadow, divine light, dark skies, celestial elements, mythological scale

Military lane:
Steel, smoke, tactical shadows, field gear, command-room energy, grounded intensity
```

## Typography Direction

Use typography that feels:

* Bold for headlines
* Readable for body copy
* Cinematic without being gimmicky
* Professional enough for retail and media use

Avoid overly decorative fantasy fonts for large blocks of text.

Decorative or stylized type may be used sparingly for accents, but readability must come first.

## Color Direction

The color system should support a dark, cinematic brand.

Suggested palette direction:

```text
Deep black or near-black backgrounds
Charcoal surfaces
Muted steel gray
Warm fire or gold accents for fantasy
Cool blue or desaturated steel accents for military thriller elements
White or off-white text for readability
```

Exact colors should be defined in the design-direction document.

## Content Integrity Rules

This brand must be built on verified or approved content.

Do not invent:

* Awards
* Reviews
* Bestseller status
* Press mentions
* Sales numbers
* Publisher claims
* Reader testimonials
* Author quotes
* Personal history
* Retail availability
* Release dates
* ISBNs
* Book metadata
* Character details

If a fact is uncertain, mark it as needing verification in project notes or omit it from visible copy.

## Brand Red Flags

Avoid the following:

* Making the site look like a generic book template
* Mixing all books together without clear genre lanes
* Overusing mythology language on the In Harm’s Way page
* Overusing military language on the Rage of Angels pages
* Presenting unverified prices as permanent
* Using fake review snippets
* Inventing awards or press mentions
* Creating spoilers beyond approved synopsis details
* Using inconsistent author names
* Using placeholder retailer links on the live site
* Adding social feeds that slow down the page or distract from book conversion

## Brand Success Criteria

The author brand is successful if visitors quickly understand:

1. Eric Deloach writes dark, cinematic genre fiction
2. The Rage of Angels universe is the epic mythological fantasy path
3. In Harm’s Way is the grounded action and military thriller path
4. His stories are connected by intense characters and impossible choices
5. Visitors know where to start reading
6. Visitors can watch trailers and follow his social channels
7. Visitors can buy or listen to books from trusted retailers
8. The site feels professional, polished, and author-owned
